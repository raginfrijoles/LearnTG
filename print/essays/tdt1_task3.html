Purpose of Multimedia Product

        The multimedia product created for this task was designed to supplement the first performance objective in my task analysis.

Performance Objective 1: When given a word problem or scenario, 7th grade students will be able to identify variables and determine their relationship as a unit rate, ratio, or percentage with 100% proficiency.

        In regards to the content of the video, the content was meant to create meaningful relationships between mathematical terms and popular culture. Younger students tend to divorce the “mathematical” world from the “real” world and rarely see how we can begin to use mathematical relationships to describe everything around us. In addition, the video attempts to create a theoretical framework of “relationships” in order to help engage students on a more creative and abstract level. All too often, students view education as a transmission process and forget that they are capable of creating their own unique knowledge.

Audience

        The audience for the video consists of 7th grade math students at a private Catholic school in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Class sizes are relatively small and never exceed fifteen students. There is a total of 37 students in the middle-school with the majority of students being of Hispanic heritage (around 70% - 80%) (personal observation). Test data from the SMI assessment revealed that half of the seventh grade class is below the 820 quantile level in mathematics. Although the average puts the class above the 820Q marker, there is a significant cluster of students (6 out of 12) that are below 820Q proficiency. This reveals that a significant portion of 7th grade students are still struggling with rates, ratios, and percentages in the context of word problems. Socially, 7th students are proficient in the newest technologies and are aware of all the latest social trends (newest smartphones, Marvel, dabbing, etc.).

Software

        For this particular video, a total of six different softwares were used to create the final product: Adobe Premiere, Adobe After Effects, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Audition, and 4k Download. This particular cluster of software was chosen mainly for each of their various specialties.

        Adobe Illustrator was used to manage various vector based graphics found at Freepik.com. Vector graphics are great to use in video production because of their ability to retain their resolution at various scale factors.

Adobe Photoshop was used to convert various JPEG images into PNG images with transparent backgrounds. Photoshop has excellent color selection and masking features which is essential for isolating images from their various backgrounds.

Adobe Audition was used to record and edit the underlying audio track. Audition provides the user with not only recording capabilities, but helpful equalization options to boost vocal ranges so that my voice would be clearly audible.

4k Download was used to capture various YouTube videos that I felt would enhance the overall experience of the video. This includes various scenes from the newer Marvel movies. 4k Download was chosen specifically for its ability to retain resolutions of 720p and upwards.

Adobe After Effects was used to animate all of the assets and text that would reside between video clips. After Effects provides various “extras” like motion blur and animated masking that gives products that extra production value.

Adobe Premiere was used because of its efficient means of organizing audio and video. It was used to make cuts and to monitor the overall flow of the final product. Premiere acted as the “master” file which housed all of the creative assets and was my export point for when the project was ready for distribution.

Process

  1. Write a script
  1. If you have a learning outcome that you want to achieve, write it at the top of your script so that you can reference it as you write.
  2. If you have an idea for a visua, put it in parenthesis or make a note on the side of your document.
  1. Create your storyboards
  1. Keep it simple. Draw stick figures, squiggles, or notes. These storyboards are not set in stone, they are your starting point.
  1. Open Adobe Audition and record your script
  1. Create a new multitrack session
  2. On the first track, hit the “R” button to activate recording preparation. This will activate your microphone so that you can test it is working properly.
  3. Click the circle recording button near the bottom of Audition and recite your script. Don’t worry about doing it all correctly all the way through. Split your script into sections and recite each one separately.
  4. Press “R” and “V” to cycle between the Razor Tool and the Selection tool. Move your clips around so that they flow nicely from section to section.
  5. Go to Effects > Filter and EQ > Graphic Equalizer (10 bands). Mess with the ranges and try to get your voice to sound as clear and vibrant as possible.
  6. When you are satisfied with your recording, go to File > Export > Multitrack Mixdown > Entire Session and save your file in the WAV format.
  1. Find and edit image/graphic assets
  1. Based on your script, you will have various images you want to use in your video. You can use Google images or vector graphics like those found at Freepik.com.
  2. If you have a JPEG that you want to isolate from the background (white backgrounds work best), open the image in Photoshop. Press “W”. This will activate the magic wand tool. Select the background with the magic wand tool then go to Select > Inverse at the top menu. Next click on the masking icon next to the “fx” button at the bottom of the layer menu. If you are happy with your newly isolated image, go to File > Export > Quick Export as PNG and save your file.
  3. If you decide to use a vector graphic from Freepik.com, open the “.ai” file in Adobe Illustrator. You can ungroup the graphic by right clicking and selecting “Ungroup”. Repeat this step and delete various sections from the graphic as you see fit for your needs.
  1. Find and download relevant videos from YouTube
  1. Download the 4k Downloader by typing in “4k Downloader” in Google.
  2. Copy the links for any videos that you would like to capture and press the “+” button in the 4k Downloader.
  3. Select what quality or format you would like to capture your YouTube video in and select a destination.
  1. Create your “master” Adobe Premiere File
  1. Open Premiere and click “New Project”. Select a destination/name for the project and select “Ok”. Go to File > New > Sequence and select the “Digital SLR > 1080p > DSLR 1080p24” setting.
  2. Import all of your assets that you DO NOT want to animate or do anything fancy with. This will include things like: audio, YouTube clips, and any other relevant videos.
  3. Press “C” and “V” to cycle between the Razor Tool and the Selection tool to move your assets around in your sequence until you have a flow that you are satisfied with.
  4. Export a rough cut in preparation for your effects work. Go to File > Export > Media and export your rough cut as an MP4 with H.264 compression.
  1. Create your effects using Adobe After Effects
  1. Create a new project by following steps similar to those found in creating an Adobe Premiere Project.
  2. Go to File > New > Composition and create a composition that is 1080p at 24fps.
  3. Import your rough cut and all of the assets that you want to animate. Drag your rough cut into your newly created composition.
  4. Drag in your assets as you scrub through your rough cut and begin animating them. You can do this by selecting an asset and pressing “P” (position), “S” (scale), or “R” (rotation) to bring up various attributes. Next, click the little stopwatch next to the attribute to set a keyframe. Scrub a few frames forward or backwards and then change your assets attribute to set a second keyframe. This will create a linear interpolation between two sets of attribute data and is the basis for the rest of your animation and effects.
  5. You can also add various effects like wipes, drop shadows, and beveling by browsing the effects menu and experimenting with the various options.
  6. Add motion blur by selecting the option checkbox next to each layer that looks like three circles traveling upwards. If you don’t see this box, click the “Toggle Switches / Modes” at the bottom of the layer menu to toggle other options.
  7. When you are satisfied with your effects, hide your rough cut layer by click the eyeball in the layer menu and then save your project.
  1. Import effects and export your project.
  1. Open your Adobe Premier project and select File > Import and select your After Effects project. Premiere will ask you which composition you want to import. Select the composition with all of your effects. Drag the effects on top of your rough cut.
  2. Reassess your video and make final cuts/adjustments based on what is working and not working.
  3. When you are satisfied with your project, Go to File > Export > Media and export your video as an MP4 with an H.264 compression codec.
